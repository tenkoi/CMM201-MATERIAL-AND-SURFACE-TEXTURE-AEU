Chapter Two · Why We Look Closely

Course Objectives

  1. Explain the basic understanding of different surfaces, materials, textures and their function.
  2. Describe how to work with sensitivity towards the surroundings, and mimic its materials and textures for application in digital space.
  3. Acknowledge the techniques of capturing photos from real life and converting them into your own materials and textures.
  4. Explain how to collect, source, navigate, select, retrieve, evaluate, manipulate and manage information from a variety of sources.

Chapter Five · The Case Study

Final Project Case Study

Featured Brief

The SDG Awareness Asset Pack

Your capstone brief: choose one United Nations Sustainable Development Goal and produce a pack of 3–5 game-ready textured props for a hypothetical SDG awareness campaign or educational game — built in Blender, textured from your own study of real-world surfaces.

The process is the point. You will walk the streets, markets and shorelines around you; photograph rust, moss, cracked earth and peeling paint; analyse what time and the environment did to those materials; and then mimic them in digital space. Every texturing decision must trace back to something you personally observed — that observation-to-pixels chain is what the project assesses (CLO 2, Analyse).

Deliverables:

  • 3–5 props with clean UVs, plus colour, bump/normal and roughness maps
  • Rendered turntables or beauty shots at 1080p, lit with three-point or HDRI lighting
  • A material breakdown document linking your reference photos to your final textures and your chosen SDG
SDG 11 · Sustainable Cities SDG 12 · Responsible Consumption SDG 13 · Climate Action SDG 14 · Life Below Water SDG 15 · Life on Land
